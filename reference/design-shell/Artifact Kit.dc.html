Landscape — Artifact Kit

A design language for what Landscaper builds on the fly.

Ad-hoc artifacts ("show me the P&L for year 6") can't map to pre-built screens — Landscaper composes them. Today that composition works but looks beneath the hand-built surfaces. This kit is the fix: a fixed anatomy + six body primitives with hard rules, so anything assembled from them looks native. Designed panel-width-first: the default render target is the right panel (~560px beside a full-width chat, like a Claude/ChatGPT artifact pane), not a full page — expanding the panel relaxes density, never changes structure.

Hard rules — not modification_spec options: numerics right-aligned, headers left-aligned, item lists always left-justified (never centered — the question goes away); KPI tile contents centered unless the tile holds a list; currency in $ with thousands separators, unit stated once in the header; one accent color; editable values always dashed-underlined; empty result = annotation primitive explaining why, never a blank table; whenever a map is involved it renders first, directly above the data it shows. Width contract: compose for 480px minimum, 560px default; panel expansion widens tables and unstacks KPI strips — it never adds or reorders sections. Workbench collapse: multi-region screens (market, dev sales) become sub-views behind a segmented switcher — one region at a time, never side-by-side (K6); master-detail column pickers become drill-downs with a breadcrumb.

The setup

Chadron Terrace is a 113-unit 1971-vintage community in Hawthorne, CA, running 90.3% occupied with in-place rents averaging $2,150/unit — 20.5% below the comp-set market rate. The rent roll (V2, 9/17/25) is the governing source; four suspect cells remain flagged against Landscaper's market estimates.

What the numbers say

The income approach carries a PV of $38.3M. The loss-to-lease of $67.5K/month is the whole thesis: the 1BR renovation scenario ($17.6K/unit, uncommitted) needs a $147/mo premium to clear a 10% return, against an observed $180–240 spread at renovated comps.

Open questions

Commercial unit 100 has sat vacant through the trailing period; the office unit's below-market lease has no recorded end date; and the Y6 tax reassessment assumption drives most of the late-projection expense growth. All three are open items on the project home.
