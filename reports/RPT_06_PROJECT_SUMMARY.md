## Section Breakdown

### Section 1: Identity & Purpose
One-line header block with:
- Project name, address, location (county/submarket)
- Property type (Land Dev / MF / Mixed Use)
- Analyst name, analysis date
- Purpose statement (Feasibility Study / Valuation Report / Acquisition Underwriting / other)

**Formatting:** Centered, bold, clear hierarchy.

### Section 2: Executive KPIs (Land Development)

Display 6 cards or a 2×3 grid:

1. **Total Project Cost** — Sum of all development costs (land, infrastructure, soft costs, contingency)
2. **Equity Required** — Funding gap not covered by debt; % of total cost
3. **Expected IRR** — Project-level IRR to developer (before waterfall); comparison to developer hurdle (e.g., 15%)
4. **Net Present Value (NPV)** — @ discount rate (e.g., 20%); absolute $ value of project
5. **Equity Multiple (EM)** — Cumulative to developer; target comparison (e.g., 2.0x over 10 years)
6. **Project Timeline** — Years from acquisition to exit/stabilization; phase count, critical path

**Formatting:** Cards with bold title, large metric value, gray subtext (target/benchmark). Color-code: green if meets/exceeds hurdle, yellow if close, red if below.

### Section 3: Executive KPIs (Multifamily)

Display 6 cards or 2×3 grid:

1. **Total Units** — Unit count; breakdown by bedroom if space allows (e.g., "135 units: 45 studios / 60 1BR / 30 2BR")
2. **Stabilized NOI** — Annual net operating income when fully leased and stabilized (Year N)
3. **Direct Cap Rate** — NOI ÷ market value; comparison to market cap rate (e.g., 4.5% vs. 4.0% market)
4. **DCF Valuation** — Present value of all future cash flows; terminal value assumptions stated
5. **Concluded Value** — Reconciled value from all three approaches (Sales Comp, Cost, Income); assignment of weights
6. **Going-in Cap Rate** — Assumed cap rate at acquisition (if applicable); relationship to stabilized cap rate

**Formatting:** Cards with metric value + subtext (units, %) + color-coding (green = strong market position, yellow = near-market, red = challenged).

### Section 8: Key Risks & Sensitivities

**Land Development:**
- **Absorption risk:** 270 units/year assumes 100% pace. Downside (200 units/yr) extends project 25 years; extends IRR by 300 bps and delays equity returns.
- **Price sensitivity:** 1% decline in price appreciation reduces IRR by 250 bps.
- **Financing cost:** Construction loan rate increase (6.5% → 7.5%) reduces project IRR by 150 bps.
- **Infrastructure cost overruns:** 10% increase in hard costs reduces IRR by 200 bps; contingency provides 9% buffer.
- **Market correction:** Recession in Year 5 (peak buildout) could compress home prices 10–15%; project IRR survives with mid-20s % returns.

**Multifamily:**
- **Lease-up risk:** 6–9 month lease-up at 95% occupancy (vs. 3% stabilized vacancy); initial years operate at 85% occupancy. First-year NOI ~$2.4M (vs. stabilized $1.86M).
- **Rent growth:** If market rent growth = 0% (flat), stabilized year NOI = $1.76M; cap rate rises to 4.7% (valuation $37.2M). Sensitivity: -$3.8M per 1% rent growth miss.
- **Operating expense inflation:** 3.0% (vs. assumed 2.5%) increases stabilized OpEx by $77K; NOI reduces to $1.78M.
- **Interest rate risk:** If permanent loan rate rises to 6.5% (vs. 5.5% assumed), annual debt service increases $85K; affects equity cash flow distribution Year 10+.
- **Market cap rate expansion:** If market cap rates rise 50 bps (5.0%), terminal value drops to $37.1M; IRR sensitivity: -150 bps.

**Mitigation strategies:**
- Phased acquisition (Land Dev): Staged equity draws reduce early-stage capital exposure
- Fixed-rate debt (MF): Lock permanent loan rate at 5.5%; eliminates interest rate risk
- Expense controls: Target 41% OpEx ratio (vs. market 42–44%); operational excellence preserves margin
- Rent growth buffer: 2.0% growth assumptions achievable in 8-year rent growth history; downside to 1.0% still supports 4.0% cap rate valuation
